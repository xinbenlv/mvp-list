I want to create a PRD for a G-Brain-based skill or pipeline. The process is meant to help families like ours, with a one-year-old baby, plan getaway trips such as a one-day Saturday itinerary.

The workflow has three core steps:

1. Curate

   We usually discover interesting places through social media or search, then organize them manually. We expect to accumulate roughly 100 to 1,000 restaurants or points of attraction.

2. Index

   After places are collected, GBrain structures them and fills in the following core information:

   (a) General information: Google Maps and Apple Maps IDs, location, hours, and basic budget.
   (b) Restaurant-specific information: popular dishes, cuisine type, and in-restaurant vibe.
   (c) Attraction-specific information: museums, national parks, and similar places should include the main sights and relevant background.

3. Trigger & Ranking

   (a) Triggering: the system automatically generates weekend suggestions every Wednesday.
   (b) Ranking: based on our state for the week, such as how busy the week looks in Google Calendar, rank the existing 100 to 1,000 restaurants or attractions. Filter out places we have already visited during ranking.
   (c) Plan generation: generate one or two itinerary options. Use two unvisited places as the main anchors, then choose between the remaining options. We can consider places we have visited and liked, or places we have not visited yet.

The input data should include:

1. Whether we have visited the place.
2. If visited, whether we liked it.

4. Output

   1. A complete agenda with the reason for picking each place and itinerary highlights.
   2. An itinerary overview image generated by the GPT Image Tool that clearly shows the map overview, main restaurants and dishes, and the main sights at the places to visit.
